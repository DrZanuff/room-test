Practical Environment Assessment

A quick note on compensation: Like most take-home assessments across the industry, this one is unpaid. Your submission will be used solely for evaluation purposes and will not be used in any commercial or business activities. We have kept the scope tight and intentional so your time investment stays minimal, and we truly appreciate you taking the time to complete it!

Assessment:

Please create a small interactive 3D environment in Godot 4.x that demonstrates physics-enabled objects and basic interaction logic. This task is intended to evaluate engineering clarity, interaction design, and project structure rather than visual polish.

The environment may represent a small room, a simple workspace, or a similar contained indoor setting of your choice. Keep the scope minimal and focused.

Required Features:

Your environment should include at least two physics-enabled objects using RigidBody3D, each capable of a basic interaction. It should also contain one interactive mechanic, such as an opening door, a sliding drawer, a trigger-based switch, or a simple state-driven interaction.

The scene should demonstrate proper collision handling, stable physics behavior, and clean structural organization. Node hierarchies should be logically arranged with descriptive naming conventions. Code written in GDScript or C# should be readable and appropriately structured.

Evaluation Criteria:

We will evaluate overall project organization, physics implementation quality, interaction clarity, code readability, and the strength of your engineering reasoning. This assessment is intended to take approximately one to two hours.

Deliverables:

Please submit a link to your Godot project. Additionally, provide a short Loom video (3–5 minutes) walking through the environment, explaining the scene hierarchy, your physics implementation, and any interaction logic.\*
